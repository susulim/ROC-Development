Indigo Apartments – Dallas, TX

Units: 1,217
Acquisition Date: June 2010
Strategy:
  • Raise revenues and reposition the asset through common area & amenity improvements
  • Reduce expenses through better on-site management controls
Status: Unrealized – Stabilization Phase

Oppurtunity

ROC|Bridge was presented with the opportunity to purchase a multifamily property in Dallas, TX from a CMBS Special Servicer. The asset was built in 1984 and is located on 9669 Forest Lane. (add map locator) The previous owner allowed Indigo to fall into a state of disrepair which caused occupancy to fall significantly. In May 2009, the property was turned over to a court appointed receiver. At the time of purchase the occupancy had fallen to approximately 45%. To reach stabilization the asset needed substantial renovation, marketing, and management improvements.

The opportunity in this transaction was to acquire an excellent asset, in an improving neighborhood, from a highly motivated lender at a significant discount, and with a competitive, interest only debt package included. ROC|Bridge’s principals planned to implement its business plan designed to effectuate the strategies outlined above, and reach stabilization by the end of the third year of ownership.

Execution

ROC|Bridge’s principals were able to successfully purchase the asset in June 2010 at 39% of estimated replacement cost. Since acquisition, ROC|Bridge has completed the following:

  • Completed $4.5 million in capital improvements. These improvements were all completed faster than expected and within the overall original budgets.
  • Security upgrades, clubhouse renovations and repositioning designed to provide comprehensive amenity and community services programs including upgrading the fitness center, adding a soccer field, improving landscaping, providing community education classes, a business center, children’s play areas, unit upgrades, and needed deferred maintenance improvements.
  • Implementing a utility reimbursement program which increased revenues substantially.
  • Reducing operating expenses substantially by renegotiating utility contracts, cutting controllable expenses, and reducing collection losses.

Results

Thus far ROC|Bridge has increased occupancy from 45% to over 83%. Stabilized occupancy is estimated to be 85%. ROC|Bridge has nearly stabilized the property a year faster than originally forecasted.

As a result of both increased rents and occupancy, and reduced operating expenses, annualized net operation income has increased from negative ($264,000) to over $2.25 million.

This asset will be positioned for sale in the 4th quarter of 2012.
