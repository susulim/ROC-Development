Cherry Creek Corporate Office

Denver, CO
Square Feet: 594,044
Acquisition Date: July 2011
Strategy:
  • Reposition the Tower through capital improvements and the addition of the parking structure
  • Modify entitlements from office to multifamily for sale of excess land
  • Utilize low interest debt to increase cash flow during entitlement and lease-up process
Status: Unrealized – Entitlement and Lease-up phase

Oppurtunity

ROC|Bridge was presented with the opportunity to purchase a property composed of four office buildings in Denver, CO. The asset is summarized below:

The opportunity is as follows:

  • Realize immediate and significant cash flows with 355,687 square feet of 100% occupied space of stable tenants, and by executing substantial expense savings.
  • Lease approximately 120,000 square feet of vacant space in the “Tower” to higher quality tenant base.
  • Convert extra land entitlements from office to multifamily and sell to a multifamily developer. This market currently has very high demand for additional “Class A” multifamily space.
  • Add value to both the Campus and the Tower buildings by constructing a parking structure close to all of the buildings as well as by completing deferred capital items and renovation work. Increase lease rates and net income in the Tower by leasing the balance of the vacant space.

Execution

ROC|Bridge purchased the asset in July 2011 at approximately 54% of the estimated replacement cost, while still receiving significant and stable cash flow. The following physical and operational changes have been completed:

  • Upgraded the lobbies, elevators, corridors, restrooms, fitness center, and cafe. Also replaced the roofs on three of the four buildings.
  • Improved tenant profile by attracting higher quality tenants. Over 54,000 square feet of new leases have been completed in less than 12 months.
  • Completed “spec” suites resulting in increased leasing momentum
  • All entitlement and pre-construction work on the re-positioning and re-zoning of the multifamily parcel have been completed.
  • Reduced projected capital outlay for parking structure and site improvements where the city government agreed to shoulder these capital improvements via a “Tax Incremental Funding” program.
  • Overall site plan has been submitted to the city government for finalization. These include the re-zoning a portion of the site to allow for residential use and the construction of a new parking structure. All designs have the support of the city government and is pending public hearings.

Results

The property currently provides a cash yield in excess of 10%.

In March 2012, ROC|Bridge entered into an agreement to sell 4.5 acres of land to a residential developer for three times the original pro forma amount. ROC|Bridge is in the process of obtaining approval from the city government to re-zone the 4.5 acre plot for residential use.
